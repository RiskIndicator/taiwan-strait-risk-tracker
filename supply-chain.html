The Supply Chain Index

A real-time measure of global trade friction. Are physical goods moving freely across the oceans, or is the system breaking down?

Why it matters: The Globalization Fracture In a stable world, shipping is cheap and energy is abundant. Geopolitical tension in major oceanic choke points (like the Taiwan Strait or the Red Sea) causes these costs to spike, driving inflation directly to your wallet.
