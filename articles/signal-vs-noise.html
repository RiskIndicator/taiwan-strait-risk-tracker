Signal vs Noise: Why We Ignore 90% of Taiwan Strait News Headlines

By Taiwan Strait Tracker Intelligence Team | February 2026

The modern news cycle is built to monetise panic. When it comes to the Indo Pacific, routine military posturing is constantly framed as the prelude to World War III.

To build a reliable OSINT dashboard, we ruthlessly strip away emotional journalism to find actual kinetic threats.

How Our NLP Engine Filters the Noise

Our tracker relies on Natural Language Processing (NLP) to bypass human bias. We categorise terminology into a strict hierarchy:

  • Level 1 (Noise): Words like "slams", "warns", and "tensions" indicate zero kinetic movement.
  • Level 2 (Elevated): Words like "deployment" and "incursion" indicate physical movement.
  • Level 3 (High Risk): Words like "blockade", "kinetic", and "mobilisation" are precursors to actual conflict.

The Ultimate Filter: Capital Markets

Capital markets act as our lie detector. If our engine detects High Risk keywords, but the S&P 500 and TSMC remain stable, the algorithm flags the news as a diplomatic bluff.
