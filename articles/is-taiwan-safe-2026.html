2. The "Military Risk" vs. Reality

Our live tracker frequently logs Chinese military incursions into the ADIZ (Air Defence Identification Zone). This sounds scary, but context is key.

The ADIZ is not Taiwan's sovereign airspace. It is a buffer zone that extends hundreds of miles offshore. When you hear about "20 jets," they are usually flying far out at sea, signalling politically rather than preparing for an attack on the city.

The Bottom Line: Life in Taipei is normal. The metro runs on time, night markets are packed, and locals are not panic-buying supplies.

3. The Real Risks (What Actually Hurts Tourists)

While war risk is statistically low for a short trip, Taiwan does have real hazards you should prepare for:

  • Traffic: This is the #1 danger. Scooter traffic in cities like Tainan and Kaohsiung can be chaotic. Always look both ways, and think twice before renting a scooter yourself.
  • Natural Disasters: Taiwan sits on the "Ring of Fire." Earthquakes are common. Most are small, but it helps to download the government's emergency alert app upon arrival.
  • Typhoon Season: From June to October, flights can be cancelled. Monitor the weather forecast.
